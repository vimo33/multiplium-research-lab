## 2. Why It Matters for Regenerative Viticulture

**Value-chain relevance:** Biome Makers operates directly at the vineyard soil layer — the foundational node of regenerative viticulture. Their BeCrop® Test uses next-generation DNA sequencing (16S rRNA and ITS markers via Illumina MiSeq) to decode the functional microbiome, providing growers with actionable prescriptions for soil health recovery, disease suppression, and input optimization.

**KPI relevance:**
- **Soil health:** Direct — BeCrop® quantifies microbial diversity, suppressive microbes, nutrient-cycling organisms, and biological disease risk indicators.
- **Carbon:** Indirect — healthy microbiome correlates with higher soil organic matter and carbon sequestration potential; BeCrop reports include carbon-relevant bioindicators.
- **Water:** Indirect — improved soil biology enhances water-holding capacity and reduces irrigation need.
- **Biodiversity:** Direct — BeCrop Sustainability module tracks biodiversity improvements over time.
- **Chemical reduction:** Direct — Napa Valley case study: customized BeCrop prescriptions generated +170% ROI vs. blanket chemical applications; Vinyes Domènech reported disappearance of Blackfoot and powdery mildew.

**Thesis alignment:** Core/direct. Soil microbiome intelligence is the diagnostic foundation for any credible regenerative viticulture practice. BeCrop is one of the few tools providing quantitative, crop-specific microbiome data with commercial-scale deployment in wine regions.

## 3. Product and Technology

**Core product:** BeCrop® platform — comprising BeCrop® Test (soil DNA analysis), BeCrop® Farm (SaaS dashboard for agronomic decision support), and BeCrop® Trials (product efficacy assessment for input manufacturers, formerly Gheom).

**Technology type:** DNA metagenomics (16S rRNA + ITS amplicon sequencing via Illumina MiSeq) + machine learning on a proprietary soil microbiome database.

**Deployment model:** Hybrid — soil sample kits mailed to labs for sequencing, results delivered via cloud-based SaaS platform. Licensing model for channel partners (DISAGRO, Midwest Laboratories, UPL).

**Software/hardware/biotech mix:** Primarily software + biotech (lab-based genomics). No hardware manufactured. The "intelligence layer" sits in the proprietary database and ML models.

**Data moat:** Publicly stated world's largest agricultural microbiome database — 55M+ taxonomic references, 200+ crops, 56+ countries, 2.2M+ acres analyzed. Network effects: every additional sample improves predictive accuracy of models. This database is the primary moat.

**Unique technical edge:** Functional microbiome analysis (not just presence/absence of taxa, but metabolic function prediction). BeCrop Sustainability quantifies microbiome-derived ESG indicators over time — differentiated from conventional soil tests (NPK-only) and early competitors (Trace Genomics, now Miraterra, which focused on pathogen-only detection).

## 4. Business Model

**Customer types:**
- Primary: Crop input manufacturers and biologicals companies (use BeCrop Trials for product validation)
- Secondary: Agronomists, distributors, cooperatives (license BeCrop to serve their grower networks)
- End customers: Growers / vineyard operators (buy BeCrop Test through channels)

**Revenue model:**
- Per-test fee (BeCrop Test): Not publicly disclosed, but inferred to be $100–$500/test range based on comparable soil DNA services
- Technology licensing: Channel partners (DISAGRO, Midwest Laboratories) license the BeCrop platform — recurring SaaS/licensing revenue
- B2B trials: BeCrop Trials fees paid by agribusiness / biological input companies for efficacy studies

**Pricing:** Not publicly disclosed.

**Sales complexity:** Moderate. Primary B2B sales (to labs, distributors, agrochemical cos.) reduce direct-farmer sales friction. Adoption requires behavioral change from growers accustomed to chemical-only inputs.

**Channel dependencies:** Heavily channel-driven. Key partners — DISAGRO (Latin America), Midwest Laboratories (US), UPL (global biologicals), Food4Sustainability (Portugal) — are critical for geographic scale. Loss of key channel partners would reduce reach.

## 7. Competitive Position

**Direct competitors:**
- **Miraterra (acq. Trace Genomics):** DNA soil analysis with pathogen/nutrient focus; acquired by Miraterra — reduced independence signal
- **EarthOptics:** Soil sensing (physical + biological); hardware-heavy
- **Continuum Ag:** Soil health analytics, US-centric
- **Indigo Ag (Biotrinsic):** Microbiome-derived seed coatings; different delivery but competing for agribusiness soil health wallet share

**Substitute solutions:**
- Conventional soil labs (NPK testing, no genomics)
- Remote sensing / satellite-based soil proxies (cheaper but no functional microbiome data)
- Agronomist-only prescriptions (no data layer)

**Moat strength assessment:** STRONG. The combination of (1) world's largest functional soil microbiome database, (2) vetted science and peer-reviewed validation, (3) multi-crop global coverage, and (4) established agribusiness channel partnerships creates meaningful network-effect barriers. Trace Genomics' asset acquisition by Miraterra removes one credible direct competitor. No other company publicly claims a comparable cross-crop, cross-geography microbiome database.

**Why this company stands out:** First-mover in functional (not merely taxonomic) microbiome analysis at commercial scale. Strong IP around proprietary database and ML models. Two distinct revenue streams (grower-facing diagnostics + agribusiness trials/licensing) provide diversification. Recognition by Fast Company and Bayer validates technical credibility.

## 8. Investor View

**Strengths:**
- World's largest proprietary soil microbiome database (55M+ taxa, 2.2M+ acres) — durable data moat
- Dual revenue model (end-grower diagnostics + agribusiness licensing) reduces customer concentration risk
- Global commercial presence (56+ countries) at Series B stage indicates strong product-market fit
- High-profile channel partners (Bayer, UPL, DISAGRO, Viking) signal credibility
- Fast Company #4 in Agriculture 2024 — brand awareness unusual for a B2B soil-test company

**Weaknesses:**
- Revenue not publicly disclosed; scale and path to profitability unverifiable from public data
- Per-test revenue model implies high transaction volume needed for scale — potential margin pressure
- Heavy channel dependency: if Bayer or DISAGRO internalize similar capabilities, Biome Makers' reach could be disrupted
- Highly scientific product requiring agronomist intermediaries — limits direct-to-grower adoption velocity

**Opportunities:**
- MRV/carbon markets: BeCrop biological data could become a standard input for soil carbon verification (Verra, Gold Standard)
- EU Farm to Fork regulatory tailwinds — microbiome testing increasingly required under regenerative certification programs
- Expansion of BeCrop Sustainability module into ESG reporting for corporates and wine brands
- Licensing acceleration post-Series B: more agritech distributors seeking validated soil biology tools

**Threats:**
- Major agrichemical companies (BASF, Corteva, Bayer) could internalize microbiome testing capabilities
- Carbon market slowdown reduces urgency of soil biology quantification
- Miraterra (Trace Genomics) relaunch or new well-funded entrants
- Regulatory fragmentation in DNA testing across geographies (export restrictions, data sovereignty)

**Scaling risks:** Volume-dependent per-test model requires large sales force or robust channel partnerships to scale economically. Lab throughput capacity may constrain growth if processing is centralized.

**Margin risks:** DNA sequencing costs are declining (commoditizing Illumina costs) but so are substitute analysis prices. Premium must be sustained through decision-value, not just sequencing novelty.

**Adoption risks:** Grower behavior change — moving from traditional soil tests to microbiome-based agronomic decisions — requires ongoing agronomist education.

**Overall investor signal: HIGH**

## 10. Exit Logic

**Likely strategic acquirers:**
- **Bayer Crop Science / BASF / Corteva / Syngenta:** Soil biology data is the frontier of digital agronomy; any major agrochemical company seeking to build a "biologicals + diagnostics" platform would view Biome Makers' database as a strategic asset
- **UPL / Arysta (UPL):** Existing partnership could evolve into acquisition to lock in the BeCrop trials network
- **Precision ag platforms (Trimble Ag, John Deere's Climate/Granular):** Adding soil microbiome to precision ag data layer
- **Carbon registries / MRV platforms (Indigo Ag carbon division, Regrow):** Soil biological data for high-integrity carbon accounting

**Financial acquirers:** Tier-1 agri-focused PE or growth equity (Pontifax AgTech, Leaps by Bayer, S2G Ventures) could seek a pre-IPO consolidation position.

**Comparable precedents:**
- Indigo Ag (raised $650M+, soil microbiome focus, though pivoted)
- Trace Genomics (~$17M raised, acquired by Miraterra — shows M&A demand for soil genomics assets)
- Pivotal / The Climate Corporation (acq. by Bayer $1.1B) — precedent for soil data platform valuations

**Why it matters strategically:** The BeCrop database will become increasingly valuable as regulatory frameworks (EU Soil Health Law, carbon credit protocols) require verifiable biological soil data. An acquirer gains not just a product but a global standard-setter in soil microbiome analysis.
